Current Research

1. Antarctic Sea Ice Concentration Biases

As a co-investigator on a NASA-ROSES funded project, I study how ocean waves in the marginal ice zone affect passive microwave sea ice concentration (SIC) retrievals in Antarctica. My work develops a framework that combines Sentinel-1 SAR, Sentinel-2 optical, and ICESat-2 altimetry to identify wave-influenced ice and quantify retrieval biases in widely used datasets such as the NSIDC Climate Data Record.

2. Preparing for the NASA–ISRO SAR Mission (NISAR)

As an Early Adopter of the NASA–ISRO SAR (NISAR) mission, I plan to combine L-band NISAR with C-band Sentinel-1 to improve sea ice classification during the melt season. This builds on my earlier work demonstrating the complementary strengths of both frequencies for monitoring summer Antarctic sea ice.

3. Arctic Langmuir Turbulence

I use coupled simulations with the neXtSIM sea ice model and WaveWatch III wave model to assess turbulence generated by waves under ice. This project provides the first Arctic-wide assessment of Langmuir turbulence potential in realistic wave–ice conditions, informing how mixing and energy exchange are represented in climate models.
