About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Brown University, with background in physical and satellite oceanography and specialization in radar remote sensing. My research focuses on the interactions between sea ice, waves, and the upper ocean, especially in the marginal ice zone (MIZ), where fragmented, drifting ice meets open water. This region is a hotspot of dynamic processes like wave-induced ice breakup and atmosphere–ocean heat and momentum exchange. These interactions are critical for sea ice evolution but remain poorly represented in most climate models. I am particularly interested in how sea ice breakup and melt influence the coupling between the ocean, atmosphere, and ice systems, and how satellite observations can reveal and quantify these complex interactions across scales.
