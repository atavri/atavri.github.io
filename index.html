Sea Ice · Waves · Remote Sensing

As a scientist and educator, I am fascinated by the physical processes that shape the polar oceans – especially the dynamic marginal ice zone where sea ice meets open water. My research focuses on sea ice geophysics and wave–ice interactions, using microwave remote sensing to improve how these processes are represented in climate models.

About Me

I have always been drawn to understanding the physical processes of the ocean surface and how they can be observed from space. My work bridges satellite remote sensing, field campaigns, and coupled climate models to study the polar oceans, with a focus on the ice edge, where waves and open water alter sea ice dynamics. I aim to build connections between detailed physical processes and their representation in climate models, using microwave sensors to study sea ice variability and wave–ice–ocean interactions. Outside of research, I enjoy mentoring, teaching, and working across disciplines to explore the complexity of our planet.
