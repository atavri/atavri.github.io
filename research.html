Research

I study how sea ice, waves, and the ocean interact across scales — from individual floes to the polar basins. I combine multi-sensor satellite data, field campaigns, and coupled models to advance our understanding of polar processes and their role in the climate system.

Waves in Sea Ice

Waves are powerful agents in the marginal ice zone (MIZ): they break, drift, and disperse sea ice, accelerating retreat and altering air–sea exchange. I use satellite radar and in situ observations to quantify wave–ice coupling and connect observed patterns to process-aware ocean models.

  • Wave-induced breakup and floe-size variability.
  • How wave–ice interactions modify surface roughness and drag.
  • Observed wave damping linked to model energy dissipation.

Microwave Scattering Across Melt

During melt, sea ice becomes a complex water–snow–ice mosaic. Multi-frequency (C & L band) SAR reveals how scattering shifts from surface ponding to volume processes. These relationships extend reliable retrievals deeper into the melt season and tie polarimetric features to albedo and energy-flux changes.

  • Field-validated links between melt ponds and polarimetric SAR.
  • Surface vs. volume scattering transitions through melt.
  • Pathways to season-robust geophysical retrievals.

Diagnosing Biases in Passive-Microwave SIC

Algorithmic differences in passive microwave (PM) sea-ice concentration (SIC) propagate into climate records. I use an observation-space approach: forward-emulate brightness temperatures (Tb) from physics-informed SIC, then compare with AMSR2/SSMIS to localize regime-dependent biases and wave-driven errors.

  • Diagnostics in Tb space (not SIC-vs-SIC) to identify bias sources.
  • Use SAR, ICESat-2, and wave hindcasts to constrain retrieval errors.
  • Improved inputs for coupled climate models and reanalyses.

Turbulence, Waves & the OSBL

Coupled neXtSIM–WaveWatch III experiments quantify how wind–wave misalignment and partial ice cover reshape mixing and Langmuir turbulence beneath sea ice. Results inform physically consistent parameterizations of the ocean surface boundary layer (OSBL) in climate models.

  • Wave-driven mixing and Langmuir turbulence under ice.
  • Relate turbulence metrics to satellite observables.
  • Forward-emulation of PM Tb from CMIP-class simulations.
