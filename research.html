Research

Combining satellite remote sensing, process-level physics, and coupled models to understand sea-ice variability and improve climate representations.

From Sea ice floes to the basin-scale retrievals

Study pan- Arctic and Antarctic processes that govern ice breakup and drive surface and lateral melt.

  • Link melt ponds/roughness/snow to scattering and thermodynamics.
  • Contrast Arctic vs. Antarctic surface processes.
  • Translate small-scale physics into basin-scale constraints.

Microwave scattering across the melt season

Polarimetric SAR (C- and L-band) separates surface/volume scattering across melt, extending usable climate records through the melt season.

  • C-band: pond onset; L-band: pond drainage volume effects.
  • Field validation (e.g., MOSAiC) links SAR to albedo/flux.
  • Next: leverage higher-frequency missions for roughness/structure.

Biases in passive radiometer retrievals

Forward-emulate brightness temperatures (Tb) from model SIC and compare with AMSR2/SSMIS to identify regime-dependent algorithmic biases.

  • SAR + ICESat-2 + wave hindcasts to locate wave-driven errors.
  • Targeted corrections for improved climate records and init.
  • Part of NASA Cryosphere effort / SCAR fellowship plan.

Turbulence, Waves & OSBL

neXtSIM–WaveWatch III simulations show how wind–wave misalignment and partial ice cover shape vertical kinetic energy and mixing under ice.

  • Quantify wave-driven mixing and Langmuir turbulence.
  • Inform mixing parameterizations in climate models.
  • Forward-emulation of Tb from CMIP-class simulations.
