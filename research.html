Current Research

Combining satellite remote sensing, process-level physics, and coupled models to understand sea-ice variability and improve climate representations.

From Floes to the Ice Pack

Variability from floe to basin scale with emphasis on the marginal ice zone (MIZ), where breakup and waves drive lateral melt and mixing. I connect floe-scale physics—roughness, melt ponds, and snow—to basin-scale constraints on heat, momentum, and freshwater exchange.

  • Link surface properties to scattering & thermodynamics.
  • Contrast Arctic and Antarctic melt-season processes.
  • Translate small-scale physics into climate constraints.

Microwave Scattering Across Melt

Polarimetric SAR (C and L band) separates surface and volume scattering through the melt season, extending the usable window of remote-sensing climate records. Field validation (e.g., MOSAiC) relates SAR signatures to albedo and energy-flux changes.

  • C-band: pond onset; L-band: drainage & volume effects.
  • Process-level links between hydrology and backscatter.
  • Next: leverage higher-frequency missions for roughness/structure.

Biases in Passive-Microwave SIC

Forward-emulate brightness temperatures (Tb) from model SIC and compare with AMSR2/SSMIS to reveal regime-dependent algorithmic biases. SAR, ICESat-2, and wave hindcasts help diagnose and correct wave-driven errors in SIC retrievals.

  • Observation-space diagnostics (Tb) instead of SIC-vs-SIC.
  • Targeted bias corrections for climate integrity.
  • Supports initialization of coupled models and reanalyses.

Turbulence, Waves & the OSBL

neXtSIM–WaveWatch III experiments quantify how wind–wave misalignment and partial ice cover shape vertical kinetic energy, mixing, and Langmuir turbulence beneath sea ice—informing physically consistent mixing parameterizations in climate models.

  • Quantify wave-driven mixing & Langmuir turbulence.
  • Relate remote-sensing observables to OSBL physics.
  • Forward-emulation of Tb from CMIP-class simulations.
